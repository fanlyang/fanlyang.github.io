SPEEDUP

Unified Acceleration across Classical, Quantum, and AI Paradigms

My core research interest lies in strong advancing the theoretical and methodological foundations strong of three complementary computational paradigms: Parallel computing, Quantum computing, and AI-driven surrogate modeling. I am particularly fascinated by their fundamental principles, the connections between them, and their potential to provide unified frameworks for addressing complex scientific and engineering challenges.

Building on my background in computational engineering, I aim to formally abstract complex systems into rigorous mathematical frameworks, investigate novel algorithms, and explore deep integration across these paradigms. This focus on foundational research, rather than solely on applications, will guide my doctoral work and drive my contributions to the underlying science of these computational approaches.
