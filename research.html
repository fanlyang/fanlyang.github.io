Research Experience

MPI Parallel Molecular Dynamics Solver

Hand-coded a production-grade MD solver in C++ (Velocity-Verlet integrator) for large-scale physical simulations; implemented MPI-based domain decomposition to balance workloads, optimizing short-range force calculations for high parallel scalability critical to high-throughput ML/AI pipelines.

Quantum-Classical Hybrid Computing for Linear Systems

Conducted a comparative study of Hybrid Quantum-Classical approaches (HHL and VQLS) for solving linear systems ($Ax=b$), investigating performance, stability, and resource trade-offs; orchestrated heterogeneous workflows using XACC, integrating quantum hardware into classical pipelines, and performed low-level benchmarking and compilation to optimize execution. The results provide insights for potential future acceleration of computationally intensive tasks.

Object-Oriented Advanced Nonlinear FEM Solver

Engineered a modular Java FEM solver with Newton–Raphson and generalized arc-length methods; designed for flexible integration of new elements and materials, enabling physics-informed surrogate modeling and robust convergence for complex nonlinear systems, supporting scalable AI-driven scientific simulations.

AI for Scientific Computation

Developing surrogate modeling techniques for uncertainty quantification and accelerating simulation pipelines using AI-driven methods, building on insights from physics-informed computational frameworks.
