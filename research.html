Research Experience

MPI-based Parallelization and Optimization of a Classical Molecular Dynamics Solver

  • Developed a molecular dynamics solver in C++ with Velocity-Verlet integration.
  • Implemented MPI domain decomposition and cell-list optimization for efficient parallelism.
  • Achieved excellent scalability on HPC clusters by minimizing communication overhead.

This project explores advanced parallel communication schemes in classical molecular dynamics simulations. The solver employs a spatial decomposition model using ghost-cell updates and asynchronous non-blocking MPI operations to minimize communication time. Benchmark tests demonstrate near-linear scaling up to 512 cores, validating both computational efficiency and domain decomposition strategy.

Quantum–Classical Hybrid Computing for Linear Systems

  • Implemented both HHL and VQLS algorithms to solve linear systems on hybrid platforms.
  • Used XACC framework to orchestrate hybrid quantum-classical workflows.
  • Benchmarked performance across simulators and quantum hardware backends.

The project focuses on hybrid quantum-classical workflows integrating variational quantum solvers with classical optimization loops. Emphasis was placed on circuit depth reduction, parameter initialization, and hardware noise mitigation. Results provide insights into the practical feasibility of near-term quantum linear solvers.

Object-Oriented Development of an Advanced Nonlinear FEM Solver in Java

  • Developed modular FEM architecture with extensible material and boundary models.
  • Implemented Newton–Raphson and arc-length methods for nonlinear structural analysis.
  • Enabled visualization and post-processing for convergence and deformation study.

The FEM solver adopts a fully object-oriented architecture featuring abstract base classes for elements, materials, and solvers. Nonlinear solution strategies were tested on buckling and post-buckling problems, achieving robust convergence and extensibility for future development.

AI for Scientific Computation

  • Developing surrogate modeling for Uncertainty Quantification (UQ) in simulations.
  • Combining classical numerical solvers with machine learning acceleration.

Ongoing research focuses on data-driven surrogate models leveraging Gaussian Processes and Neural Operators to approximate complex PDE solutions. The aim is to accelerate uncertainty propagation in multiscale simulations.
